Sri Lanka · 7-Day Guide

A 7-Day Sri Lanka Journey,
Done Right

The perfect week — ancient kingdoms by morning, misty hills by evening, and a south coast close that feels like a reward earned.

By Destinova Journeys 7 min read Sri Lanka

Seven days is not a long time. But in Sri Lanka, seven days done properly — without rushing, without group tours, without wasted hours — can feel like a lifetime of experience compressed into the most beautiful week imaginable.

Day 1 – 2

The Cultural Triangle — Where History Breathes

Begin in the north-central heartland, where Sri Lanka's ancient civilisations left behind monuments so grand they still stop you mid-breath. Sigiriya — the 5th-century rock fortress that rises 200 metres from the jungle floor — is best at dawn, when the mist sits low and you have the frescoes almost entirely to yourself.

The afternoon belongs to Polonnaruwa, the island's medieval capital, where enormous stone Buddhas carved directly from rock face the forest in quiet contemplation. There is something about standing before a figure that has existed for 800 years that recalibrates everything.

"Sigiriya at dawn — mist in the valley below, frescoes above, and the feeling that you have the whole ancient world to yourself. That is Sri Lanka done right."

Sigiriya Rock Fortress · UNESCO World Heritage Site

Day 3

Kandy — The Last Royal Capital

The road to Kandy winds through rubber plantations and spice gardens, past roadside stalls piled with king coconuts. The city itself sits around an artificial lake built by the last Kandyan king — beautiful and slightly melancholy, as all last capitals tend to be.

Destinova Insider
Temple of the Tooth — Visit at Puja Time

The temple holds three puja ceremonies daily at 6:30am, 9:30am and 6:30pm. The evening puja — with drummers, dancers, and the scent of jasmine — is the most atmospheric. Arrive 30 minutes early with a private guide to secure the best viewing position.

Days 4 – 5

The Hill Country — Tea, Mist & Slow Time

The train from Kandy to Ella is, by most accounts, one of the most beautiful rail journeys on earth. Book a first-class observation carriage, pack breakfast from your hotel, and watch the island transform from lowland heat into cool, fragrant elevation.

Days 6 – 7

The South Coast — Where the Journey Ends Well

The south coast is Sri Lanka's exhale. Tangalle, Mirissa, Weligama — small bays strung together by a coast road lined with coconut palms, fishing boats painted in primary colours, and the kind of restaurants where the fish was swimming that morning.

Spend your final days with no itinerary. Sleep in. Walk the beach barefoot. Order a rice and curry so good you'll spend months trying to recreate it at home. That is the south coast's gift — it makes you stop.
