Before Booking a Kids Club —
Read This

Not all kids clubs are created equal. After years of visiting them across Sri Lanka and the Maldives, here is what actually separates exceptional from disappointing.

By Destinova Journeys 8 min read Sri Lanka & Maldives

A good kids club can be the difference between a family holiday that works for everyone and one that works for no one. But not all kids clubs are created equal — and the brochure photographs rarely tell you what you actually need to know.

If you are planning a family holiday to Sri Lanka or the Maldives and a kids club is part of the plan, this is the article to read first. After years of arranging family journeys across both destinations, we have sat in enough of these clubs, spoken to enough staff, and heard enough feedback from returning families to know exactly what separates the exceptional from the merely acceptable — and the acceptable from the actively disappointing.

◆
The First Question to Ask

What Is the Club Actually For?

Kids clubs exist for two reasons: to give children a genuinely good time, and to give parents the freedom to enjoy the holiday without guilt. Both are legitimate. The problem arises when a resort treats the kids club primarily as a marketing asset — something that photographs well in brochures but operates on minimum staffing, minimum programming, and maximum screen time.

The very first thing to ask when evaluating a kids club is not what activities are on offer, but what the club's philosophy is. Does the resort treat children as guests in their own right — with genuine attention and staff who chose this work because they love it? Or are children an afterthought, parked safely while the real hospitality happens at the adults-only pool?

"The best kids clubs are not babysitting services with craft tables. They are genuinely joyful places where children choose to be — even when their parents come to collect them."

The right kids club creates experiences children carry home with them

What to Look For

The Six Things That Actually Matter

01 · What to Look For Staff-to-Child Ratios

This is non-negotiable. For children under five, the ratio should be no higher than 1:3. For older children, 1:5 or 1:6 is acceptable for structured activities. Anything beyond that and no child is getting meaningful attention.

Ask the resort directly. Do not accept vague reassurances about "appropriate supervision." A specific number is a sign that the resort takes this seriously. A deflection is a red flag.

Genuine attention requires the right ratio · Not all resorts meet the standard

02 · What to Look For Age-Appropriate Programming

A good kids club does not run the same programme for a three-year-old and a twelve-year-old. The best clubs have clearly separated age groups — toddlers, juniors, and teens — with activities designed for each developmental stage.

For younger children: sensory play, water activities, simple cooking, age-appropriate storytelling. For older children: snorkelling lessons, cooking classes with real recipes, cultural activities, nature programmes. For teenagers, the best clubs offer real experiences — learn-to-surf sessions, behind-the-scenes kitchen tours, photography walks, marine biology introductions.

03 · What to Look For Cultural Content

In Sri Lanka and the Maldives, a kids club has a remarkable opportunity to introduce children to genuinely different cultures, histories, and natural environments. Children learn to identify endemic bird species. They are introduced to the stories behind Buddhist temple art. They learn to make string hoppers alongside local staff who take visible pleasure in sharing it.

These experiences are what children talk about when they get home. Not the pool. Not the iPad in the corner. The afternoon they made a coconut lamp with the lady who taught them three words in Sinhala.

Ask the Resort This

Does the kids club have any Sri Lanka or Maldives-specific programming? Can you share an example of an activity that children could not do anywhere else in the world? The quality of that answer will tell you everything.

04 · What to Look For The Physical Environment

Visit the club before you book. If you cannot visit in person, ask for a video tour or recent photographs — not the marketing shots, but the actual space as it operates day to day.

Look for: natural light, outdoor access, clean and well-maintained equipment, spaces that feel designed for children rather than simply designated for them. A kids club in a basement or a repurposed meeting room is telling you something.

Spaces designed for children — not merely designated for them

05 · What to Look For Flexibility and Family Integration

The best family resorts understand that children do not always want to be separated from their parents — and parents do not always want to be separated from their children. The best kids clubs offer flexible participation: structured morning programmes alongside free-access afternoon sessions, family activity options, and the ability for parents to join in when they want to.

Beware the club that operates exclusively as a drop-off service with rigid hours. Family travel works best when the structure serves the family, not the other way around.

06 · What to Look For Evening Options

For many families, the evening is the critical time. Parents want dinner together, ideally without the pressure of keeping small children entertained across a three-course meal. The best resorts offer evening kids club hours, children's dining times, or dedicated family dining areas where the service is genuinely family-oriented rather than merely tolerant of children.

Sri Lanka vs Maldives

Different Destinations, Different Strengths

Kids Clubs in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka's luxury properties are generally excellent at integrating children into the broader cultural experience of the destination. The best family properties combine high-quality kids club facilities with curated family activities that make use of Sri Lanka's unique environment: wildlife encounters, cooking classes, village visits, elephant sanctuary experiences.

The island is also genuinely safe and friendly for children. Sri Lankan culture is deeply hospitable to families, and children are welcomed warmly almost everywhere. The rice and curry at a local restaurant is an adventure, not a risk.

Forest School · Cape Weligama · Nature-led learning on the south coast of Sri Lanka

Kids Clubs in the Maldives

The Maldives offers something unique: the ocean itself is the greatest kids club there is. Snorkelling with reef fish and turtles, learning to paddle a traditional dhoni, watching the bioluminescence at night — these are experiences that change children. The best Maldives resorts build their kids programmes around marine education and water confidence.

Look for resorts with dedicated marine biology programmes for children, beach clean-up activities (which children find genuinely engaging), and junior diving or snorkelling certifications for older kids.

The Maldives · Where the ocean itself becomes the classroom

"The Maldives taught my daughter to snorkel. She is seven. She came home knowing the names of fish I cannot identify. That is what the right kids club looks like."
