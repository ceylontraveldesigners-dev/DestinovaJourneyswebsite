An Island of Two Worlds

Fourteen Days Through Sri Lanka's Wild Heart & Ancient Soul

Sri Lanka is one of the world's great surprises — an island where ancient stone kingdoms and misty tea highlands exist alongside some of Asia's finest wildlife encounters. This fourteen-day journey traces a path from the pristine forests of Wilpattu, through the legendary rock citadels of Sigiriya and Polonnaruwa, across the sacred city of Kandy and the cool highland estates of Nuwara Eliya, and down into the elephant plains of Udawalawe before arriving at the colonial grandeur of Galle's UNESCO-listed fort. Every destination curated by Destinova Journeys to show you the island at its richest and most revealing.

Day 01 Arrival · Negombo

Pearl of the Indian Ocean

Welcome to Sri Lanka. Upon your arrival at Bandaranaike International Airport, you are greeted by your private Destinova Journeys chauffeur guide and escorted to your vehicle. The transfer to Jetwing Beach in Negombo takes just thirty minutes along the coast — your first taste of the island's warm air and tropical light.

Negombo is a gentle introduction to Sri Lanka: a sun-drenched coastal town with a working fishing harbour, Dutch colonial canals, and a long stretch of golden beach. Check into your hotel, settle in, and allow the island to ease you into its unhurried rhythm. Tonight, the sound of the ocean and the warmth of a first Sri Lankan sunset are the only agenda.

Days 02 — 03 Wilpattu · Wild North

Into the Forest — Sri Lanka's Secret Wild

After breakfast, check out and transfer north to Wilpattu National Park — a park quite unlike any other in Sri Lanka. Unlike the open grasslands of Yala, Wilpattu is characterised by deep forest cover: dense dry zone woodland and thorny scrub, vast open grasslands, and the remarkable natural lakes known as villus — saucer-shaped basins that fill with rainwater and attract extraordinary concentrations of wildlife. The park's remoteness has kept it wild and uncrowded, making every encounter feel like a genuine discovery. On arrival, check into your lodge and head out for your first evening game drive as the park shifts into its magnificent golden hour.

Day 3 is given entirely to Wilpattu's rhythms: a dawn safari at first light, when leopards and sloth bears move through the cool morning air, and a dusk drive as the villus fill with birdlife and the light turns amber. The stretch of coast nearby — including the dramatic Portuguese Bay and Dutch Bay, with their reddish cliffs rising from tiny beaches — adds a final, unexpected dimension to this remarkable landscape.

Days 04 — 05 Sigiriya · The Ancient Cities

The Lion Rock & the Lost Kingdoms

After breakfast, check out and drive to Sigiriya. Rising dramatically from the central plains, the Sigiriya Rock Fortress is perhaps Sri Lanka's single most dramatic sight — a 5th-century royal citadel built by King Kashyapa atop a volcanic plug of rock 200 metres above the surrounding jungle. The ascent takes you past the world-renowned frescoes of the Heavenly Maidens, painted in earth pigments directly onto the rock face, and through the Lion's Gate — the gateway through which the last king once looked out over his kingdom. The views from the summit stretch to the horizon in every direction.

Day 5 takes you to Polonnaruwa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and Sri Lanka's magnificent mediaeval capital from the 11th to 13th centuries. The ruins here are among the finest in Asia — the Gal Vihara's colossal rock-cut Buddha statues, the great dagobas, the palace complexes and council chambers of an ancient civilization at the height of its power. A knowledgeable local guide brings it all vividly to life.

Day 06 Kandy · Sacred Capital

The Temple, the Lake & the Living City

After breakfast, check out and drive to Kandy — Sri Lanka's cultural capital and the seat of the last Sinhalese kingdom. En route, pause at the Dambulla Cave Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site where five caves shelter over 150 Buddha statues and some of the most extraordinary ancient murals in Asia, painted across ceilings and walls in a continuous scroll of colour and devotion that stretches back fifteen centuries.

On arrival in Kandy, check into your hotel and devote the afternoon to the Temple of the Tooth Relic — the most sacred Buddhist shrine in Sri Lanka, housing a relic believed to be the Buddha's tooth. Set around the shores of Kandy Lake, the temple's gilded shrines, drifting incense, and the resonant sound of ceremonial drums create an atmosphere of exceptional stillness and reverence. The city itself rewards a gentle evening walk: Buddhist temples, Hindu kovils and colonial-era churches standing in easy coexistence along the same street.

Days 07 — 08 Nuwara Eliya · Hill Country

Little England in the Clouds

The drive to Nuwara Eliya climbs steadily into the cool highlands, the landscape softening from tropical green to rolling tea gardens as the altitude rises. Nuwara Eliya — known affectionately as "Little England" for its colonial bungalows, rose gardens, and cool mountain climate — is one of Sri Lanka's most distinctive destinations. Check into Jetwing St. Andrews and allow the crisp highland air to refresh after days in the heat of the lowlands.

Day 8 begins at Horton Plains National Park — Sri Lanka's highest and most dramatic plateau, a wind-swept landscape of montane forest, grasslands, and mist. The trail to World's End delivers one of the island's great experiences: a precipitous 900-metre escarpment dropping away to the southern plains below. In the afternoon, visit a working tea estate and factory to follow the journey of Ceylon tea from freshly hand-picked leaf to the perfectly brewed cup — a process as skilful and precise as any art form.

Days 09 — 11 Udawalawe & Sinharaja · Southern Wild

Elephant Country & the Last Rainforest

Check out and descend through the foothills to Udawalawe National Park, framed by soaring highlands to the north and one of the world's finest destinations for seeing wild Asian elephants. Largely grasslands and bush forest, the park hosts large resident herds who move freely across the landscape in family groups, allowing for extended, unhurried viewing. An afternoon safari on arrival offers your first close encounters. En route, pause at the Udawalawe Elephant Transit Home — a remarkable facility dedicated to rehabilitating and returning orphaned elephant calves to the wild.

Day 10 continues south to Sinharaja — one of Sri Lanka's most extraordinary and least visited destinations. The Sinharaja rainforest occupies a deep ridge at the heart of the island's wet zone and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site: a pristine, ancient forest covering a broad ridge, accessible only on foot with expert park rangers. The biodiversity here is staggering — over half the endemic birds of Sri Lanka can be found in a single morning walk, along with rare mammals, extraordinary insects, and a canopy of trees hundreds of years old. Day 11 brings another full morning in this irreplaceable wilderness before continuing towards the south coast.

Days 12 — 13 Galle · Fort & South Coast

The Fort at the Edge of the World

After breakfast, check out and drive to Galle — a journey that traces the southern coastline through fishing villages and coconut groves before arriving at one of the most remarkable towns in Asia. Galle Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the best-preserved colonial sea fortress on the continent — built by the Portuguese in the late 16th century, expanded by the Dutch, and finally polished by the British, its ramparts, bastions, and grid of narrow streets create a living town that has changed little in three centuries. Check into Heritage Galle Fort and spend the afternoon exploring on foot.

Day 13 is entirely at leisure within the fort and along the south coast. Walk the ramparts at sunset as the Indian Ocean catches the evening light on three sides. Browse the boutiques, galleries, and spice shops that line the old Dutch streets. Eat fresh seafood at a table overlooking the sea. The fort rewards wandering — every alleyway reveals something: a hidden courtyard, a centuries-old church, a craftsman at work in a doorway. A perfect final full day before departure.
