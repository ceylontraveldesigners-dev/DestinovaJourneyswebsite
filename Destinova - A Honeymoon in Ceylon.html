Days 02 — 03 Sigiriya · Cultural Triangle

The Lion Rock & Village Soul

After breakfast, your chauffeur begins a scenic four-hour drive to Sigiriya — a journey through lush paddy fields, roadside temples, and the gentle rhythms of rural Sri Lankan life. On arrival, check into Lake Lodge Kandalama, where floor-to-ceiling windows frame a landscape of lake and forest. In the afternoon, make the iconic ascent of Sigiriya Rock Fortress, an ancient royal citadel rising dramatically from the surrounding plains. The summit — reached after a climb of approximately 2.5 hours — rewards with sweeping views that stretch to the horizon.

Day 3 brings you deep into the life of the Sri Lankan countryside. In Hiriwadunna, a warm village welcome leads to a tractor ride through the jungle where you discover traditional chena cultivation — age-old farming methods, crops guarded from wild elephants, and treehouses under the stars. A peaceful canoe glides you across the village lake to a local home, where you join in preparing Sri Lankan curries over a wood fire before sitting down together to eat. In the late afternoon, a guided cycle ride through the village offers one last, unhurried look at everyday life.

Days 04 — 05 Kandy · Cultural Capital

Sacred City & the Art of Kandy

A two-hour drive through the hill foothills brings you to Kandy — Sri Lanka's cultural heart, its last royal capital, and one of the most atmospheric cities in all of South Asia. Afternoon is devoted to the Temple of the Tooth Relic, one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in the world, where a relic believed to be a tooth of the Lord Buddha resides within a sequence of gilded shrines. The atmosphere is deeply spiritual: incense, offerings, devotees in white, and the soft resonance of chanting — a profoundly moving experience. In the evening, a vibrant Kandyan cultural show brings traditional dance and drumming to vivid, colourful life.

Day 5 is a walking day in the finest sense. A knowledgeable local guide leads you around the scenic Kandy Lake — its surface mirroring the Temple on the opposite bank — past the historic British Garrison Cemetery, through streets where Buddhist temples, Hindu kovils, Catholic churches, and mosques stand shoulder to shoulder in easy coexistence. Pause at the Empire Café before winding through the lively local market, rich with colour, spice, and the particular energy that only real markets carry.

Days 06 — 07 Haputale · Hill Country

Mist, Tea & the Quiet of the Hills

A 2.5-hour drive climbs steadily into Sri Lanka's hill country, leaving the heat of the lowlands behind as the landscape transforms into a world of cool air, rolling green tea plantations, and mist that drifts between the ridges. Haputale is a charming town perched at elevation, with views that stretch across mountain ranges to the coast on clear days. Check into the Wishing Tree — an intimate boutique property set amidst the tea estates — and simply breathe.

On Day 6, visit a working tea factory to witness the full journey of Ceylon tea — from the tender hand-picked leaf to the perfectly brewed cup. Each step of the process reveals something of the care and craftsmanship that has made Ceylon tea legendary the world over. Day 7 is left entirely for leisure: the cool climate, the panoramic views, and the unhurried pace of the hills are the only agenda. Perhaps a morning walk through the mist, an afternoon on the veranda, and a long, quiet evening together.

Days 08 — 09 Yala · Wilderness

Wild Nights & Starlit Dinners

A four-hour drive south brings a dramatic shift in atmosphere — from the cool highlands to the warm, wild south, where Yala National Park spreads across a vast landscape of dry scrub, rocky outcrops, and ancient lagoons. Home to the world's highest concentration of leopards as well as elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and a spectacular array of birdlife, Yala delivers the kind of encounters that live in the memory for a lifetime. Your first evening game drive sets the tone: the park at golden hour, a jeep moving quietly through the bush, and the extraordinary sensation of being a guest in the wilderness.

Day 9 begins with a guided morning walk through a nearby village — a peaceful, grounding experience that connects you to the people who share this landscape with its wildlife. After breakfast and a potter's morning, the afternoon holds a creative highlight: a hands-on pottery experience with a local artisan, shaping clay on a spinning wheel to create something entirely your own. The day ends with pure romance — a BBQ dinner set in the jungle, a bonfire, a canopy of stars, and the sounds of the bush around you.

Days 10 — 13 South Coast · The Long Beach

Four Days of Pure, Honeymoon Bliss

A three-hour coastal drive sweeps you westward along Sri Lanka's southern shore — a shoreline of golden coves, fishing villages, and the Indian Ocean glittering to the horizon. Check into Apa Villa and let the beach take over. Four days here are designed to have no agenda whatsoever: swim in the sea, take long sunset walks along the shore, indulge in spa treatments, sleep in, and simply exist in the luxury of time and togetherness.

The south coast is Sri Lanka at its most unhurried — far from the tourist trail, blessed with clean water and unspoilt coastline, and warmed by long golden evenings. Apa Villa's intimate setting provides the perfect backdrop for the final chapter of your honeymoon: quiet mornings, private dinners, and the soothing rhythm of the ocean as a constant companion.
