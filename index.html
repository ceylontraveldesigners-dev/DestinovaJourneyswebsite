Our Commitment

Travel That Leaves the World
Better Than It Found It

At Destinova, responsible travel is not a product feature or a page on our website. It is the standard against which every journey we design is measured.

Sri Lanka and the Maldives are not simply beautiful destinations. They are living ecosystems, ancient cultures, and fragile environments that deserve more than extractive tourism. The way a journey is designed — where you stay, how you move, who benefits from your presence — determines whether travel is a force for good or a force for erosion.

Every itinerary Destinova designs is held to a standard that goes beyond luxury. These are our commitments.

01 · Low-Impact Movement

Moving Through the World
Without Leaving a Scar

All ground transfers are arranged in hybrid or electric vehicles wherever available. Where road journeys would be long and high-impact, we recommend helicopter transfers — which reduce both road-hours and exhaust time significantly.

Every Destinova journey is fully paperless. No printed itineraries, no printed vouchers, no unnecessary documentation. All briefing packs, day plans and supplier communications are digital.

We do not provide single-use plastics on any Destinova-managed transfer or experience. Guests are provided with refillable bottles at the start of every journey. We also advocate a slow travel philosophy — fewer destinations visited more deeply means fewer transfers and a lower overall footprint.

02 · Wildlife & Ocean Ethics

The Animal's Experience
Comes First. Always.

All Destinova safaris operate under a strict silent protocol — distance maintained, engines switched off, no pursuit. Our guides and naturalists are chosen because they understand that a witnessed leopard simply existing in its environment is the entire point.

We actively promote guest participation in the Whale Shark Research Programme in the Maldives — one of the most important citizen science projects in the Indian Ocean. In both destinations we facilitate coral restoration and replanting activities through our resort partners.

On Sri Lanka's south coast, we support sea turtle monitoring programmes at Rekawa and Tangalle. All elephant encounters we facilitate are observation or sanctuary-based only — no riding, no performance, no exceptions.

03 · Community & Economy

Every Journey Should Leave
Something Behind

Every Destinova itinerary directs spending toward locally owned properties, family-run restaurants and artisan cooperatives wherever possible. All our guides and drivers are locally employed, fairly compensated, and chosen because they grew up in the communities they interpret.

We build village and artisan encounters into our itineraries not as optional extras but as core experiences. A single Destinova guest visiting a lacquerware craftsperson generates more direct income than a week of market sales. We ensure that income is fairly distributed and that the craft traditions involved are supported, not commodified.

We advocate a carbon contribution programme at the point of booking — not mandatory, but explained and offered to every guest as a way to offset the environmental cost of long-haul travel.

04 · Green Property Curation

We Only Recommend Properties
That Earn It

Sustainability is part of our property selection criteria — not a checkbox. A property that is physically beautiful but environmentally careless does not make our collection. We look for solar or renewable energy use, plastic-free operations, locally sourced food, community employment, and active conservation programmes.

We work closely with collections and properties that share this standard — and we visit them ourselves before we recommend them to you.

Active Programmes

Conservation You Can
Be Part Of

Maldives

Whale Shark Research Programme

Guests snorkel alongside whale sharks under researcher supervision, contributing photo-identification data to one of the Indian Ocean's most important marine science projects. No touching, no feeding — pure observation and data collection.

Sri Lanka & Maldives

Coral Restoration & Replanting

Working with resort marine teams, guests participate in coral fragment cultivation and reef restoration. Coral frames are maintained on-site; returning guests can check on the growth of coral they planted on a previous visit.

Sri Lanka · South Coast

Sea Turtle Monitoring

At Rekawa and Tangalle, nesting sea turtles come ashore between November and April. Guests join trained monitors on evening beach walks — observing nesting females and hatchling releases under strict no-light, no-touch protocols.

Our Partners

Sustainability Is a
Partnership

We work with property collections whose sustainability programmes are genuine, verifiable, and ongoing — not marketing language.

Sri Lanka

Jetwing Hotels

Sri Lanka's benchmark for eco-certified hospitality. Jetwing Vil Uyana — built on reclaimed paddy land restored into a functioning wetland sanctuary — is the clearest expression of their philosophy: that luxury and ecological restoration are not in conflict.

Resplendent Ceylon

Operators of Wild Coast Tented Lodge (solar, zero-waste, wildlife corridor at Yala), Ceylon Tea Trails (estate produce, carbon-sensitive), and Cape Weligama. Each property is designed around its natural environment rather than imposed upon it.

Uga Escapes

A Sri Lankan-owned collection with deep community employment roots and active conservation partnerships at Ulagalla (ancient paddy landscape) and Yala. Small-scale by design, significant in local impact.

Tri Lanka

Off-grid eco-luxury in the hill country — rainwater harvesting, solar power, organic kitchen garden. One of the few properties in Sri Lanka that is genuinely self-sufficient in energy and water.

Maldives

Soneva

Industry-leading zero-waste operations at Soneva Fushi and Soneva Jani. The Soneva Foundation funds reef restoration, community education and renewable energy projects. On-site glass studio recycles all glass waste into art and tableware.

Six Senses Laamu

Plastic-free certified. Active coral restoration, sea turtle monitoring, and manta ray research partnership. Their resident marine biologist runs weekly conservation briefings and guest participation sessions.

Four Seasons Landaa Giraavaru

Home to the Maldives' most active marine discovery centre and an official Manta Trust research partnership. Guests can assist with coral propagation in the on-site nursery and join manta identification surveys.

Gili Lankanfushi

No shoes, no news, no plastic — a philosophy embedded in every aspect of operations. Active reef monitoring programme, regular ocean clean-ups, and some of the most comprehensive coral restoration work in the north Malé atoll.
